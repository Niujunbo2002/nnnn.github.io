About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. student in EECS at Peking University, advised by Wentao Zhang and Bin Cui. I collaborate closely with Shanghai AI Laboratory, OpenDataLab, especially with Conghui He and Bin Wang. Previously, I was a research intern at ByteDance Seed and Shanghai AI Laboratory. I received my B.Eng. degree from Beihang University, EE (GPA 3.85/4.00, Rank 1 / 203).

My research interests lie in Large Language Models, World Model, and Foundation Models:

  1. Large Language Models (2026.3-∞): I have been especially focusing on Long-Horizon Agentic RL, including the co-optimization of RL algorithms and infrastructure.

  2. World Model (2023.9-∞): my recent work has mainly centered around the OVO series, especially OVO-Bench.

  3. Foundation Model (2024.9-2026.5): my work has mainly centered around the MinerU series (56.4k GitHub stars), including MinerU 2.0, MinerU 2.5, and MinerU Diffusion.

  1. MinerU 2.0: A End-to-End Vision-Language Model for High-Speed and High-Precision Document Parsing
    J Niu*, Z Liu*, Z Gu*, and 7 more authors
    Foundation Model System Release
    Abs Blog Code Website

    MinerU 2.0 integrates a small-parameter, high-performance multimodal document parsing model for end-to-end high-speed and high-precision document understanding. With fewer than 1B parameters, it surpasses traditional 72B-level vision-language models in parsing accuracy, supports multilingual OCR, handwriting recognition, layout analysis, table parsing, formula recognition, and reading-order sorting, and reaches peak throughput above 10,000 tokens per second with sglang acceleration on a single NVIDIA 4090 GPU.

  1. Native Visual Understanding: Resolving Resolution Dilemmas in Vision-Language Models
    J Niu*, Y Zheng*, Z Miao, and 7 more authors
    Foundation Model CVPR 2026 (Findings)
    Abs PDF Code

    A framework for native-resolution visual encoding that preserves fine-grained visual cues and improves open-source VLMs on high-fidelity visual understanding tasks.
